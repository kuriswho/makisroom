Cleaning

My philosophy is very simple regarding cleaning. Little and often makes the job easier, and I try to maintain a positive attitude about the task. Many people may view keeping their home clean as a chore, but for me it is a matter of self-respect. I am a human being and I deserve to live in a clean and comfortable home. For me it is a means of self-empowerment - I cannot control my social class and I am limited in that I cannot afford an ostentatious house, but I can keep my home clean and tidy and therefore I never feel deprived when living in a small space. In fact, it may be easier to keep well than a larger space.

If you can see the virtue of it but dread the process, I hope I can help you find ways to make cleaning easier in this article. Once you have a routine, it becomes an almost medatative process and can even be enjoyable. I find the process soothing now, especially as I find that the state of my home corresponds quite closely to the state of my brain.

Reducing the Workload

Obviously, prevention is the best cure. I recommend maintaining a shoes-off rule in your home. If you live in a western country where this isn't already custom, it can be difficult to implement but I think it's worth insisting on. Guests can be encouraged by the addition of a seat inside the front door to make shoe removal easier, as well as perhaps some washable guest slippers if your floors get cold.

Another easy way to minimize mess is to also minimize what you actually own. There is already plenty online about the practise of Minimalism - I can't stress enough how much it helps to have fewer extraneous possessions to create clutter. Any trinkets I do have I keep behind glass, or in sealed boxes to allow for effortless dusting - all my surfaces are flat and empty, and therefore a quick wipe with a damp cloth will usually be sufficient to remove dust on a daily basis.

An area that it is especially important to stay on top of is the shower. If you have a shower screen, I recommend keeping a squeegee in the shower and using it every time you shower to rinse down the glass and the tiles. A proper weekly clean will be much easier without a build-up of soap scum.

Where to Invest

While I wouldn't particularly worry about buying branded cleaning products for surfaces or floors, one place I do recommend splashing out is laundry detergent. Finding a detergent you like which gets things clean in one wash at a lower temperature will pay for itself in laundry bills very quickly. Other than this, some baking soda, lemon juice, vinegar and a cheap bleach and glass cleaner are usually enough for the average home.

One place it will certainly pay off to invest a little extra is your vacuum cleaner. You will use it almost every day, and an ineffective vacuum can be frustrating as well as hard on your back. I have had little success with cordless vacuums, even the very expensive ones and so I cannot recommend them. My own vacuum is a Miele C3 of some sort and has made such a difference to the ease with which I keep my floors clean. I recommend prioritizing suction power and parts durability when looking for a vacuum cleaner. Make sure also to adjust the stem according to your height so that your back remains straight when vacuuming.

Areas Not to Forget

  • Clean your dish drainer and underneath it at least once a week
  • Wipe dust from radiators, especially if you use them to dry your clean clothes
  • Change out your household textiles (towels and sheets) at least once a week
  • Skirting boards can gather dust - wipe them with a damp cloth
  • Pull out large furniture and vacuum under it regularly - you never know what lost items you might recover
  • A thorough weekly clean of the inside of the microwave is a must to avoid a fire hazard
  • If your hob has a splashback (i.e. against a wall) the wall will need to be cleaned regularly to avoid dust clinging to grease as this becomes impossible to remove
  • Clean your fridge regularly and remove any gone-off food. A dirty fridge is less effective at keeping things fresh.

Things to Avoid

  • Never put dishes back into the cupboard unless they are properly cleaned and dry - dirty dishes stacked with clean ones mean they are all unclean, and they will also make the shelves sticky. This is very unhygenic.
  • I shouldn't have to say this but please don't allow your pets onto the kitchen counters!
  • Do not leave anything in the sink (kitchen or bathroom) when you're finished - build-up is tougher to remove
  • Try not to put things off until later - your future self will thank you for not allowing work to pile up
