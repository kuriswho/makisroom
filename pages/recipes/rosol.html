Rosół:

I used to make this with my landlord. It's very cozy. Depending on how big your pot is and how confident you are that you can lift a few litres of boiling liquid, this recipe may call for a friend.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Whole, raw chicken, sans tits
  • 4 Carrots, halved lengthways
  • 2 Large onions, halved
  • 2 Parsnips, halved lengthways
  • 3 Celery stalks
  • 5 Cloves garlic
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • Handful of peppercorns
  • A few sprigs each of parsley and dill
  • 2 tsp ground turmeric
  • Polish thin noodles
  • A few drops of Winiary Przyprawa liquid seasoning

Method:

  1. Put the chicken in the biggest pot in your house and cover with water
  2. Bring to a boil on the hob and add the carrots, onions, parsnips, celery, and garlic - You can let the chicken simmer first but we were lazy and just threw it all in at once
  3. Simmer for an hour, skimming regularly
  4. Add in the peppercorns and bay leaves and simmer for another hour
  5. Add the turmeric and simmer for a further 15 mins or so
  6. Strain the broth. This is a two person job - we recommend fishing for large removable bits first to ease some of the weight.
  7. Bone the chicken and shred the meat with a fork
  8. Store the chicken separately and add to the soup when serving
  9. Separately cook a portion of noodles
  10. Place noodles into a serving bowl, ladle over plenty of broth
  11. Add chicken and garnish with parsley and dill
  12. Season to taste with salt, pepper and Winiary Przyprawa
