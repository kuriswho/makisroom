Gołąbki:

Cabbage dumplings - this is a Polish recipe but different variations are popular in many countries.
Ingredients:

  • 1 head of Cabbage
  • 500g Pork Mince
  • 1 Large Onion
  • 1 Egg
  • 70g Rice
  • 400g Tinned Tomatoes
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 Cloves Garlic
  • 1tsp Paprika
  • 1tsp Marjoram
  • 1tsp Sugar
  • Salt & Pepper

Method:

  1. Cook the rice until it is light and fluffy - can be done ahead of time and allowed to cool
  2. Remove the core and outer leaves from the cabbage - wash it
  3. Blanche cabbage in boiling water with a little sugar and salt
  4. Meanwhile, melt the butter in a dutch-oven over medium heat
  5. Finely dice the onion and garlic and add to the pot - cook gently until transparent
  6. Place the cooked rice and 1/2 of the onion/garlic in a large bowl and add the pork mince, egg and seasonings. Mix well
  7. Preheat the oven to 160C
  8. To the onion left in the pot, add sugar and tinned tomatoes and stir to combine. Remove from heat
  9. Remove the cabbage from the water-bath. The leaves should be soft and come away easily. Lay some out and trim the hard spines
  10. Take a handful of the meat filling and roll it into a ball. Place on a leaf of cabbage, fold the sides in and then roll
  11. Place the gołąbki seam-side down in the dutch-oven, spooning a little tomato sauce over them
  12. Cook with the lid on for around an hour
