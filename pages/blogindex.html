2022

03.09

I guess I'm trying to die again in two weeks.

I spent today putting things in order for Christine and the Flower Club, so that somebody can take over my duties with relative ease. I made a list of possible loose ends that will need to be tied by my family.

It's funny. My whole life I've felt like a burden and here I am preparing to die and STILL doing everything in my power to make it as easy for everyone else as I possibly can.

I didn't mind orienting my life around other peoples' comfort but even having done that I was always viewed more like a parasite than a benefit to the people I wanted to support. I held Katie back and only when she got cancer did she recognize that I was kind of the same thing. I feel like I've lost so much in the last decade, between school friends dying and loved ones leaving and now my home and all my hopes for the future. I want to be the one leaving, this time.

The one thing I feel real remorse about is Ben, because I don't know how to take care of him going forward.

I never got over Joe saying I wasn't worth the money I cost to keep alive, or Veronica making it clear she could only love me when I was a high achiever. My best has never been enough for anyone, not even myself. No matter how hard I tried, and I tried very hard, it was never enough to get anywhere.

I can't have somewhere stable to live until I have a good job and a husband. I can't have a good job unless I pass exams that I can't pass without somewhere stable to live. I can't have a husband because he doesn't want me and I'm too old to find anyone else, and it's too urgent.

It's one think to say I have plenty of time, but even if I let go of the timeframe I wanted I still have to wake up every day in the meantime. I still have to live every waking moment in acute distress with no way to relieve my own pain and no way to seize any power over my situation.

It wasn't for lack of trying. I went out straight away and got a job, started a course, tried to make alternative plans. It wasn't what I wanted for myself but I did it, and I gave it everything I had.

I can't keep it up. If it were easy, if I were comfortably coasting with no ambition and no idea what the future held maybe I could have done it indefinitely until I figured something out, but it's NOT easy. Every day of my life is miserable and I can't keep putting one foot in front of the other when it HURTS.

Conversely, if I knew what I was even walking towards - if I still had my future with S to look forward to I could have weathered any hardship just to know I was working towards something concrete.

I've reached a point where I'm so tired of dragging my own aching heart along a pitch-black path to absolutely nothing good that I can't even envision any future now that might make this worth doing. I've gone too far for saving. Even if someone could guarantee that in a week's time I'd have a perfect life with my own home and a family and a job I was good at and plenty of free time for my hobbies, I think I'd still give up. I can't wait that long and I can't imagine anything that would be worth it anymore.

I guess keep that in mind if you are someone from my life and you feel guilty. You can rest easy knowing there was nothing you could have done. I just want the pain I'm in, that I've pretty much always been in, to end right now forever. I'm tired of being resilient.

I give up.

04.08

Well, S is gone. I'm grieving him, our house, our children and our life together.

Reading the most recent entry stings a lot now. I thought I had set my sights low - I didn't think I was hoping for too much but it would have been perfect to me. I thought that finally, God had looked at my life and witnessed my suffering and how hard I had been trying and said "yes, you deserve to have a happy life."

I thought the pain I've always felt was finally going to be worth it.

I don't understand. I am too small to see God's plan for me but if it's not this, then I don't understand why He placed it before me only to take it all away. If there is some kind of test to be passed, I don't think I'm up to it.

I slit my wrists on Thursday, rather unsuccessfully. I'm moving into homelessness this week and I'm not sure what it will involve.

I have always been someone for whom the most vital thing is my sense of home. All I've ever needed is somewhere permanent and private that is entirely my own. I don't need it to be big or beautiful, but I need somewhere that can never be taken away.

This is my worst fear on earth and God is going to make me face it. I'm petrified.

I've been just awful the last few days. My friends have been so kind and patient and I have been very ungrateful and rude. I don't want the help they can offer. I want my life with S and I want him to be my husband, I want to spend my life raising our children and learning new skills and hobbies together. I want to wake up next to him when I'm 80 and anything short of that is an insult. People have been incredibly generous and I've thrown it back in their faces.

I'm trying to curb it now, because I love my friends and I've never been someone who gives up easily no matter how much I want to. I'm feeling dreadful right now, but I will try my best to act in a way that would make me proud going forward. It is insincere for now, but it might not always be.

Migrating and making the final site updates before I become homeless.

I'm frightened, but I'm unable to kill myself so I have no choice but to keep marching forward, not knowing where I'm headed.

I'll do my best but I've been doing my best for so long and I'm so tired.

04.02

I need something to hope for. I'm struggling with responsibility, with work and with keeping myself in check socially. I can't stop making insinuations without grace, because I'm angry and hopeless and frightened.

What can I bear to hope for, now? When I envision an "ideal" future I keep catching myself reeling it back in the name of realism. I can't dream of a big kitchen because I will never be that wealthy. I can never dream of high ceilings. What would I really want, if I was unconcerned with impossibility?

I don't know, and I don't know how to find out because it hurts too much, but here's something I could hope for.

Myself and S, a living room with a comfortable couch. Enough counter space that our child (we have a child,) can help me fold dumplings in the kitchen on a weekday afternoon. A bedroom that gets natural light. Shoes lined up neatly at the door, or in a cabinet. A neat bathroom.

I still cycle and run. So does he. We are teaching our child to ride a bike. We are still in love.

I am not such an open wound of a person. I have enough love and self-respect not to pass down this insecurity to our child. I seldom worry that I'm messing everything up and would be better off dead. I seldom cry myself to sleep and when I do, S is there and strokes my hair. I do the same for him, but seldom. He has enough love and self-respect to understand that he deserves to be here, to be happy.

Our child never has to doubt that we care for them. We have enough money never to have to doubt that we can care for them.

I can walk under a gray sky with my groceries and not wonder if life will always be so hard, if the constant worry is digging trenches into my forehead every waking moment, because the constant worry has gone.

I have something that I'm good at, that I trust myself to do consistently and do well. A well-honed skill. I might work in the field, or I might not. I'd like to, but I'd like to have had a choice.

If I get even half of this my life will have been worthwhile.

2021

02.08

I've been restless and dissatisfied for months and I can't seem to snap out of it, or put my finger on what it is I'm missing. I feel like a fuck up for what happened with Mk, and although I don't want to go back on setting my boundaries, I feel like even if I did want to I couldn't. It's strange never knowing how to talk or act anymore. I don't know how to behave like a sensible adult. I want to be messy and stupid and childish, but I want also to do the right thing. I want to tell him how much I care and how much this is hurting me, but that's irresponsible and puts him under pressure and we can't have a healthy household dynamic with that added complication.

One thing I hate about how my thoughts work is that it's never enough for me to just experience a situation and have my feelings about it. I always have to relate it to the world at large, or some greater question of human nature. I'm never just having a bad day, the world is hurtling towards destruction. I'm never just feeling lonely — humanity has begun to lose touch and connection is ever more difficult on a global scale. Put simply, I'm melodramatic.

I'm still trying to reconcile the tension between my persistent feelings of otherness and my certainly romanticised but also sincere love for other people. I want more than anything to be at one with humanity and there are times I feel that very deeply, but when I'm with people I usually feel so alienated and unconvincing. I'm in this constant state of flux and I can't be comfortable anywhere for long. I guess I'm lucky to have enough time on my hands to take myself apart and reassemble every day or this would be really debilitating.

I feel like I keep getting stuck in this abyss of nuance, like I have beliefs but every point of view can be argued and everything is true/untrue some of the time, but that if I don't make sweeping generalisations it becomes impossible to talk about anything in a meaningful way. "This is true, except for when it isn't." Makes me wonder what point there is in saying anything. I've become so tired of hearing people and I hate that because it makes me feel like an asshole, one of those boring misanthropes that thinks it's cool not to care about anything. That's not me, but I just can't muster the interest lately. I don't like it at all.

I've decided to shit my brains onto the internet for lack of anywhere better to put it that doesn't hurt anyone. I never intend to hurt anyone, and that's about all I can say for myself right now.

2017

11.04

My personality seems to change a lot and it frightens me that I don't seem to have any defining traits — I can be very kind but I can also be infinitely cruel in certain circumstances and I tend to cycle wildly between polar opposites of behaviour.

I used to worry that this meant I wasn't a real person but rather just the sum of every interaction I've ever had and this was terrifying and dehumanizing for me.

Steafan pointed out that maybe not having an eternally consistent personality doesn't mean that I'm fake or non-existent. It's just a consequence of being a 3-dimensional person and perhaps makes me very real indeed.

It's only hitting me now how much this perspective has helped me to relax on my eternal and agonizing (and more tha likely pointless) quest to dissect my intangible self. I've stopped worrying so much about "not knowing who I am" and I've begun to accept that maybe this is all me.

I'm kind and cruel, driven and lazy, childish and mature and it's all real. Steafan said he sees me as "a glass half-full."
